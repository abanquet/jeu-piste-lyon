Chacune reflète à sa manière l'esprit de la cuisine lyonnaise : généreuse, conviviale et attachée aux produits de qualité. Ces établissements rendent la gastronomie accessible, tout en perpétuant so héritage culinaire.

Le Nord Brasserie - Bocuse

An iconic figure in French gastronomy, Paul Bocuse was born in Collonges-au-Mont-d'Or, near Lyon, in 1926. A three-star chef for over 50 years, he left a lasting mark on 20th-century cuisine by combining tradition and innovation.

In Lyon, he left his mark not only on the plates but also in the streets: he founded four brasseries located at the four cardinal points of the city — Le Nord, Le Sud, L'Est, and L'Ouest. Each reflects in its own way the spirit of Lyonnaise cuisine: generous, convivial, and committed to quality products. These establishments make gastronomy accessible while perpetuating his culinary legacy.
